One of the beautiful eye opening insights I got from studying arabic poetry literature, is the secret why the "Hamasah" genre has emerged, well, before stating it, a historical prologue will go a long way.

# The Hamasah genre

In the first third of the 8th century ~= 220 AH (835 CE), the renowned poet Abu Tammam, considered by some scholars to be one of the three pillars of the arabic poetry (along side Al-Buhturi, and Al-Mutanabbi), after being blocked in [Hamadan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamadan) due to a winter storm, he decided to write a special type of books, a collection of short parts from famous or unsung poems, he named it after it's first chapter ("Al-Hamasah") roughly translated to valour, which was the tradition of those times, naming the books by it's first chapter's name, same as [Kalīla wa-Dimna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kal%C4%ABla_wa-Dimna) fables collection, worth mentioning that the editors of the [Hamasah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamasah) page missed this nuance, when writing about this genre:

It doesn't only contain chivalrous odes, it has also love passages, verses discussing good manners, and others more.

Following Abu Tammam, famous poets and scholars mimicked Habib and created they're own versions of it, namely Hamasat Al-Buhturi (< 284 AH), Hamasat al-zurafa (Poems of the Refined and Witty) by Al-Abdalakani al-Zawzani, and [many more](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamasah):

![image](/assets/img/hamasah_assets/image%201.png)

One important thing to note, is that this is not the first work of literature where a collection of poetry was assembled together (although beit very close to the start of the written form tradition which was in the 7th century CE due to the [revelation of the Quran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_literature#:~:text=Arabic%20literature%2C%20primarily,%5B3%5D)), there was actually many books written in that sense, for example [Mufaddaliyyat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mufaddaliyat) ~= 145-168 AH (762-784 CE) by [Al-Mufaddal ad-Dabbi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Mufaddal_ad-Dabbi), [Asmaiyyat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asma%27iyyat) by [Al-Asmai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Asma%CA%BFi) 122–213/218 AH (740–828/833 CE), Jamharat Ash'ar al-Arab by Abu Zayd al-Qurashi's who died at 170 AH (786 CE), and many more, worth mentioning that the [Islamic golden age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_Golden_Age) is traditionally understood to have begun during the reign of the Abbasid caliph Harun al-Rashid  ****169 AH – 193 AH ****(786–809 CE), that's when the urbanization of islamic society reached it's highest rate of growth, let's plot these overwhelming numbers on a line and see if we see a pattern:

# Social Acceleration

Welcome to the "[Social acceleration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_acceleration)" phenomenon, a sociological concept pioneered by [Hartmut Rosa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hartmut_Rosa), it describes how with the recent technological advancement and globalisation of our society, a pace that our ancestors used to live with, have became not enough for us to keep up with such changes, a linear time that manifested in recurring events like seasons or day-night cycles become insufficient, and an abstract time has emerged with a different slope, depending on the necessity of the person, and it's environment, mostly sharper, as our vibrant society becomes more radioactive.

In the same framework of Rosa, I would like to append (maybe Rosa has suggested it but I didn't read it's writings extensively) that this social acceleration phenomenon is a recurring event, with each empire rise —think of Ibn Khaldun's [four stages of empire](https://www.philosopheasy.com/p/ibn-khalduns-four-stages-of-empire) theorem—, which fits nicely with my proposition, that the hidden reason behind the appearance of the "Hamasah" genre, is the social acceleration that got brought to action after the civilization explosion the Islamic region had encountered in the late 7th century CE, the Hamasah genre was a [shorter form of entertainment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_acceleration#:~:text=Additionally%2C%20social%20acceleration%20is%20evident%20on%20streaming%20services%20and%20social%20media%20trends%2C%20intensifying%20the%20enormous%20pressure%20to%20stay%20current%20with%20ever%2Devolving%20content%20and%20interactions.) that the nature of that abstract time has required, with more sophisticated societies comes faster forms of entertainment consumption, in our time, before it was books, then journals, then cinema, films, now it's reels, shorts, tiktoks, all over the place.
